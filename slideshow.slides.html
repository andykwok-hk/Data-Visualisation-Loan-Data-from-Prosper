Investigate Loan Data From Prosper¶

by Andy Kwok¶

Investigation Overview¶

We will investigate if employment status has relationship with the rate of charged off / defaulted loan. We will also investigate other variables such as home ownership and prosper rating to see if those factors will affect the borrower rate.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others.

Not Employed and Retired group are having higher defaulted and charged off loan¶

Comparing not employed and retired group with other group employed and full-time (as well as part-time, self-employed group etc. Not shown here for simplicity), Not Employed group have got the highest Charged off rate and the Retired group comes the next highest. And Retired group have got the highest default rate.

Home Owner receive better Borrower rate¶

If a customer owns a house, they would receive a lower borrower rate (at around 0.183) than those who do not (around 0.203).

Prosper Rating has critical influence on Borrower Rate¶

The prosper rating affects the borrower rate. With higher rating, the borrower rate is lower. However, within each rating, we can observe that monthly income does not affect the borrower rate.
